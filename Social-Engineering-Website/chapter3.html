Chapter 3: Online Scams

What are Online Scams?

Online scams are tricks used by criminals to get money or personal information over the internet.

These scams can happen on:

  • Websites
  • Emails
  • Social media
  • Online marketplaces

Scammers often pretend to be trustworthy so that people feel safe interacting with them.

How Do Online Scams Work?

Online scams usually start with a message, advert, or offer that grabs your attention. The scammer may try to build trust or create urgency so that you act quickly.

For example, they might:

  • Offer something at a very low price
  • Ask for help with an urgent situation
  • Pretend you owe money or need to make a payment

Once they have your trust, they may ask for money or personal details.

Common Types of Online Scams

There are many types of online scams, but some are seen more often than others.

Shopping scams happen when fake websites or sellers offer goods that never arrive. Romance scams involve someone building a relationship online and then asking for money. There are also investment scams, where people are promised large returns with little risk. Another common example is a scam message asking for help, pretending to be a friend or family member in trouble.

Key Warning Signs

  • Deals that seem too good to be true
  • Requests for money, especially in unusual ways
  • Messages from people you do not know
  • Pressure to act quickly
  • Requests to keep things secret

If you notice any of these signs, take a step back before responding.

How to Stay Safe

  • Take time to think before making any payment
  • Only buy from trusted websites or sellers
  • Be careful when talking to people you do not know online
  • Never send money or share details just because someone asks
  • Check with a friend or family member if something feels unclear

What to Do if You Think You Have Been Scammed

If you think you may have been scammed, it is important to act quickly. Contact your bank as soon as possible and explain what has happened.

You should also change your passwords to help protect your accounts. It may help to speak to a trusted friend or family member for support.

Final Thought

Online scams are becoming more common, but staying calm and taking time to check can keep you safe. If something does not feel right, it is always better to pause and ask for help.
