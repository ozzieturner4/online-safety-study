Chapter 4: Staying Safe

Why Staying Safe Matters

Scams are becoming more common, especially online. While it is not possible to avoid every risk, there are simple habits that can greatly reduce your chances of being targeted.

Staying safe is about being aware, taking your time, and knowing when to stop and check.

Everyday Safe Habits

Building a few simple habits into your daily routine can make a big difference:

  • Take your time before responding to messages or requests
  • Be careful about what personal information you share online
  • Only use websites and services you trust
  • Keep your devices updated when possible
  • Log out of accounts when using shared devices

Protecting Your Personal Information

Your personal information is valuable and should be kept private. This includes things like passwords, bank details, and addresses.

Try to use strong passwords that are different for each account. Avoid sharing personal details unless you are certain who you are dealing with.

If someone asks for information unexpectedly, it is always safer to pause and check first.

Taking Your Time

Many scams rely on rushing people into quick decisions. You might feel pressure to act straight away, but this is often a warning sign.

It is always okay to stop, think, and come back to something later. A genuine company or person will not mind if you take your time.

Asking for Help

If you are ever unsure, you do not have to deal with it alone. Talking to someone you trust can help you make the right decision.

This could be:

  • A family member
  • A friend
  • Your bank
  • A trusted organisation

Getting a second opinion can often help you spot something you may have missed.

What to Do if Something Feels Wrong

If something does not feel right, trust your instincts. It is better to be cautious than to take a risk.

You can:

  • Stop responding to the message or caller
  • Contact the organisation directly using trusted details
  • Speak to someone you trust
  • Report the situation if needed

Taking action early can prevent problems from getting worse.

Final Thought

Staying safe online is not about knowing everything. It is about being careful, taking your time, and asking for help when needed. By following simple steps and trusting your judgement, you can stay in control and protect yourself from scams.
