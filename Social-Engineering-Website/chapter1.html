Chapter 1: Introduction to Social Engineering

What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is a type of scam where criminals try to trick people into giving away personal or financial information.

Rather than hacking computers, these scammers target people directly. They might pretend to be someone trustworthy, like:

  • A bank worker
  • A delivery company
  • A government organisation
  • Even a friend or family member

The goal is simple: to get you to share information, send money, or click on something unsafe.

Why Do Scammers Use Social Engineering?

Social engineering works because it plays on human nature. Scammers know how to:

  • Create a sense of urgency (for example, saying your account will be locked)
  • Build trust by pretending to be official
  • Cause fear or worry to make people act quickly

Anyone can be targeted. It is not about being bad with technology. These scams are designed to catch people off guard.

Common Examples

  • A phone call claiming to be from your bank asking for your details
  • An email saying you have won a prize and asking you to click a link
  • A text message about a missed delivery with a link to reschedule
  • Someone online pretending to need help or money

Key Warning Signs

  • You are being rushed or pressured
  • Someone asks for personal details such as passwords, PINs, or bank details
  • The message feels unexpected or unusual
  • It sounds too good to be true

If something does not feel right, it is always best to pause and check.

Simple Tips to Remember

  • Never share passwords or PINs
  • Take your time and do not rush
  • Check with the company directly using a trusted phone number
  • Ask a friend or family member if you are unsure

Final Thought

Social engineering scams rely on tricking people, not breaking technology. By staying aware and taking a moment to think, you can protect yourself.
